Investment lending done properly

Three pillars of a smart investment loan.

We look beyond the first purchase and design your loans so they support the next property, and the one after that.

Cash‑flow & buffers

We stress‑test your scenario for rate rises, vacancies and maintenance so you are not caught off‑guard.

Scenario modelling helps decide between interest‑only vs principal & interest and how large a buffer to keep.

Loan & security structure

We generally avoid cross‑collateralisation so each property can stand on its own as much as possible.

Standalone lending gives you more control when selling, refinancing or restructuring in future.

Lender sequencing

Different lenders view rental income, existing debt and credit scores differently.

We help you choose the right order of lenders so your borrowing power lasts longer.

Using equity without over‑stretching.

Equity can be a powerful tool, but only when it is used deliberately. We help you determine how much is sensible to release and how it should be structured.

  • Calculate usable equity across your existing properties, factoring in lender LVR limits.
  • Decide whether equity should fund deposit only, or deposit plus costs and a buffer.
  • Consider future moves (e.g. renovations, future PPOR upgrade) before locking in structures.

Illustrative scenario

An investor with a $900k home (loan $540k) wanted to buy a $650k investment property. We refinanced the home to 80% LVR, released equity to cover 20% deposit plus costs, and set the new investment loan as interest‑only with full offset. This preserved cash‑flow while keeping their owner‑occupied debt on principal & interest.

This is a general example only, not advice. Your borrowing capacity and suitability are assessed individually.
