Making sense of your repayments

How to interpret your home loan repayment estimate

The repayment estimate on this page is a general illustration only. It can help you understand how loan amount, interest rate and term interact, but it does not take into account your full financial situation, lender fees or policy differences.

Repayments over time

Principal-and-interest repayments are typically calculated on a 25–30 year term. Early in the loan, a larger portion of each repayment goes towards interest rather than principal. As the loan balance reduces, the interest component falls and more of each repayment pays down the amount you owe.

This calculator uses a standard amortisation approach and assumes the interest rate stays the same for the full term, which is unlikely in practice.

Limits of online estimates

The figures shown do not include lender fees, government charges, different repayment types (such as interest-only) or changes in interest rates. They also don’t confirm your ability to obtain credit, which is assessed under responsible lending and other regulatory obligations.

This information is general in nature and is not financial product advice, tax advice or a recommendation to apply for any credit product. You should consider obtaining independent advice that is tailored to your circumstances.
