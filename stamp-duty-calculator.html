Stamp duty in context

What to know about government fees when buying property

Stamp duty and related government charges are set by each state and territory. This calculator provides an indication only based on current public thresholds and does not replace the official calculators provided by revenue offices.

Why figures can change

State and territory governments review duty scales and concessions from time to time, particularly for first home buyers and new builds. As a result, the actual amount you pay may differ from this estimate, especially if legislation or eligibility criteria have changed.

Always rely on the official calculator or written advice from the relevant revenue authority for final amounts, and check whether additional fees (such as foreign purchaser surcharges) apply to your situation.

General information only

This tool does not take into account your full circumstances, such as prior property ownership, eligibility for grants, residency status or whether you are buying as an individual, trustee or company. It is designed to give you a starting point when planning your purchase budget.

Nothing on this page is financial, tax or legal advice. You should confirm all amounts with your conveyancer, solicitor, accountant or the relevant government authority before committing to a contract.
