When refinancing can make sense

  • Your fixed rate is expiring and you want to avoid “loyalty tax” on the revert rate.
  • You have equity but your current lender isn’t competitive or flexible.
  • You are juggling multiple high‑interest debts and want one structured solution.
  • You want to fund renovations, investments or business needs using your home equity.

Smarter debt consolidation, not just “rolling it all in”.

Consolidating high‑interest debts into your home loan can dramatically reduce monthly repayments, but it needs to be done thoughtfully to avoid stretching bad debt over too many years.

  • Review all existing debts, interest rates and remaining terms.
  • Compare “do nothing”, refinance only the home loan, and full consolidation scenarios.
  • Set a clear plan to pay the consolidated portion down faster than the standard term.

Illustrative scenario

A client with a $630k home loan at 6.65%, $18k credit card and $25k car loan was paying over $4,000 per month. By refinancing to a sharper rate and consolidating the additional debts, their total repayments dropped by ~$900 per month, while a separate “debt clearing” strategy was set up to ensure the consolidated portion was paid down within 5–7 years.

This is a general example only and not personal advice. We will assess whether this approach is suitable for your circumstances.
