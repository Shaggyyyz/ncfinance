Making extra repayments work for you

How additional repayments can change the life of your loan

This calculator shows, in broad terms, how putting extra money towards your home loan can reduce total interest and shorten the term. It assumes a consistent rate and extra repayment pattern, which may not match real‑world conditions.

Compounding interest in reverse

When you pay more than the minimum required amount, you reduce the outstanding balance faster. Because interest is calculated on a lower balance, more of each subsequent repayment goes towards principal, which can create a compounding effect in your favour over time.

The results here are illustrative only and assume the extra repayments continue unchanged for the life of the loan.

Cash‑flow and suitability considerations

While paying extra can reduce interest, it may not always be appropriate if it leaves you without an adequate buffer for unexpected expenses. Lenders and advisers will consider your broader goals, savings and other commitments when discussing repayment strategies.

The information on this page is general and does not take into account your objectives, financial situation or needs. You should seek personalised advice before changing your repayment arrangements.
