Top 5 Deposit Myths for First-Time Homebuyers

The 20% Deposit Myth

The traditional advice has always been to save a 20% deposit. While this avoids Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI) and gives you immediate equity, the reality of modern property prices makes this a steep hurdle for many first home buyers.

  • Paying LMI (5-10% deposit): You can buy with as little as 5% genuine savings. The bank charges a one-off insurance fee (LMI) which is typically added to your loan balance.
  • Government Guarantee Schemes: The Home Guarantee Scheme allows eligible buyers to purchase with a 5% deposit without paying LMI. The government guarantees the remaining 15%. Places are limited and price caps apply.
  • Family Guarantee: If your parents own property, they can use a portion of their equity as security for your loan. This allows you to borrow 100% of the purchase price (plus costs) and avoid LMI completely.

Myth #2: Only 20% Down Payment is Required

Saving 20% on a $700,000 property means putting away $140,000—plus stamp duty and conveyancing costs. In a rising market, the time it takes to save that extra 10% can sometimes mean you pay more for the property itself than you would have paid in LMI.

  • Traditional 20% deposit requires significant savings and avoids LMI, but it's not the only option.
  • Alternative options like government-backed schemes and family guarantees can make home ownership more affordable.

Myth #3: Government Schemes Are Limited

The Home Guarantee Scheme allows eligible buyers to purchase with a 5% deposit without paying LMI. The government guarantees the remaining 15%. Places are limited and price caps apply.

  • Government-backed schemes like the Home Guarantee Scheme provide alternative options for buyers with limited deposits.
  • These schemes are designed to make home ownership more accessible and reduce financial barriers for first-time buyers.

Myth #4: You Must Own a Property to Use Family Guarantee

If your parents own property, they can use a portion of their equity as security for your loan. This allows you to borrow 100% of the purchase price (plus costs) and avoid LMI completely.

  • Family guarantee requires parental equity, but it's a viable option for buyers without significant savings.
  • This strategy allows first-time buyers to enter the market without paying LMI or a large deposit.

Myth #5: You Can't Use Any Other Strategies

There are multiple strategies available for first-time buyers, including government-backed schemes and leveraging family equity. These options provide flexibility and can help buyers achieve their goals without overpaying.

  • Explore all available strategies to find the one that best fits your financial situation and goals.
  • Consult with a mortgage broker to understand the options available and make informed decisions.

Conclusion

Dispelling deposit myths is crucial for first-time buyers navigating Melbourne's housing market. By understanding alternative options like LMI, government schemes, and family guarantees, buyers can make informed decisions and achieve their home ownership goals.

Whether you're considering a 5% deposit with LMI or leveraging family equity, there are multiple pathways to entering the market. Stay informed, explore your options, and work with experts to find the best solution for your needs.
