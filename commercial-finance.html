Getting the structure right from day one.

Commercial lending can be structured in many ways across entities, securities and loan splits. The right structure can improve tax efficiency, asset protection and borrowing capacity over time.

  • Clarify which entity should own the property or business (personal, company, trust, SMSF, etc.) in consultation with your accountant.
  • Decide how much security to offer and whether to keep residential property separate where possible.
  • Balance interest‑only vs principal & interest terms based on your stage of business and risk appetite.

Illustrative scenario

A trading business leased a warehouse and wanted to purchase the premises for $1.4m. We arranged a commercial loan in a new trust with the business as tenant, using both the property and limited residential security. The facility was structured with a split term loan and smaller working‑capital line, providing flexibility for future growth without over‑committing personal assets.

This is a general example only and not advice. Every commercial deal is assessed on its merits, security and serviceability.
