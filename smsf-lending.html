Who SMSF lending is typically suited to

SMSF lending is a specialist area and not right for everyone. We help you understand where it can fit, and when a different approach might make more sense.

Professionals & business owners

Often looking to have their SMSF own their practice premises while the business pays rent.

This can align business needs with long‑term retirement planning when structured correctly.

Established SMSF trustees

Members with a meaningful balance and regular contributions wanting to diversify into property.

We ensure the borrowing level is appropriate given age, contributions and fund objectives.

Trading businesses

Businesses wanting long‑term control over premises, with the SMSF as landlord and the business as tenant.

Rent must be commercial and arm’s‑length; we help lenders understand the full picture.

Long‑term investors

Members who value discipline and are comfortable with a long‑term, illiquid investment inside super.

We stress‑test cash‑flows for interest rates, vacancies and changing contribution levels.

Core SMSF lending concepts

Structure, compliance and cash‑flow.

SMSF loans sit inside a very specific framework. We focus on explaining the structure clearly and making sure the numbers work before anything progresses.

Limited recourse

SMSF loans are typically limited recourse, meaning the lender’s rights are largely restricted to the property held in the bare trust.

This influences LVRs, interest rates and which lenders are available.

Bare trust & documentation

A separate custodian/bare trust holds the property on behalf of the SMSF for the life of the loan.

Correct documentation is critical, so we liaise with your SMSF specialist throughout.

Contributions & rental income

Lenders consider employer/member contributions and rent flowing into the fund when testing serviceability.

We help ensure projections are conservative and sustainable over time.

How we guide you through SMSF lending.

  1. Initial feasibility – we discuss your SMSF balance, contributions, timeframe and goals to see whether an SMSF loan could be appropriate to investigate further.
  2. Strategy & structure – together with your accountant/adviser, we confirm the preferred structure (fund, trustee, bare trust, potential corporate entities).
  3. Lender selection & approval – we compare available SMSF lenders, outlining LVRs, rates, fees and documentation, then manage the application through to approval and settlement.

You should always obtain independent financial and tax advice before your SMSF invests in property.

Illustrative scenario

A couple in their mid‑40s with a combined SMSF balance of $650k wanted their fund to purchase a $750k commercial suite for their practice. We worked alongside their accountant to put in place a corporate trustee and bare trust, arranged an SMSF loan within typical LVR limits, and structured repayments so they aligned with rent and ongoing contributions.

This is a general example only and not personal advice. Every SMSF lending scenario must be assessed individually for suitability and compliance.
